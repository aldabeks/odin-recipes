Pikliz Recipe

Description

Pikliz is a cornerstone of Haitian cuisine, used to add heat, acidity, and crunch to balance rich and fried foods like griot (fried pork), tassot (fried beef or goat), or fried plantains

Pikliz isn’t just for flavor — the vinegar and peppers help cut through oily dishes and aid in digestion.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups shredded cabbage
  • 1 cup shredded carrot
  • 2 red bell peppers, thinly sliced
  • ½ medium red onion, sliced
  • 3 scallions, thinly sliced
  • 1 Jamaican Scotch bonnet pepper
  • 3 tablespoons whole allspice
  • 4 peppercorns
  • 2 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1½ cups white vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice

Steps

  1. Stuff cabbage, carrot, peppers, onion, allspice, cloves, and bay leaf into mason jar with lid until it`s filled to the brim.
  2. Add vinegar, and lime juice and press on the ingredients
  3. Seal the jar and shake once.
  4. Refrigerate for 2 days for flavors to meld
  5. Now you can enjoy your delicious pikliz.
