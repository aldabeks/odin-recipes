Arepas Recipe

Description

Arepas is a type of flatbread made of ground maize dough stuffed with a filling. It is mostly eaten in South America, Columbia, and Venezuela. It is also present in Bolivia, Ecuador and Central America.

The carbohydrates present in Arepas, provides energy.

Ingredients

  • 2½ cups milk
  • 1½ cups arepas flour
  • ½ cup grated Monterey Jack cheese
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • vegetable oil
  • 1 (6 ounce) cooked chicken
  • ½ Monterey Jack cheese
  • ½ avocado

Steps

  • Bring milk to simmer in a pot
  • Remove from heat and stir in butter
  • Put the arepas flour, grated Monterey Jack cheese, kosher salt, and black pepper in a large bowl.
  • Add the hot milk mixture and honey. Stir until combined.
  • Let the mixture sit until milk is abosorbed enough for soft dough to form 1 to 2 minutes. Dough will continue to stifen.
  • Form dough into balls. Flatten between palms.
  • Heat oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium heat.
  • Fry the arepas until lightly golden brown.
  • Slice each arepa in half crosswise and stuff a portion of chicken, sliced Monterey Jack cheese and avocado between the halves.
